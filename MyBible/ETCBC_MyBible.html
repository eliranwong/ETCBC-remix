Search on Lexeme or Lexeme_&_Morphology

Locate lexeme:
First, searching for morphological data alone is not helpful if searches are not connected with lexemes. Typing Hebrew letters in mobile devices is fine, but typing Hebrew vowels in mobile devices is difficult, if not impossible. Therefore, 9236 lexemes in ETCBC 4c data are assigned with numbers from 70001 to 79236 for easier typing. In this MyBible collection, it is easy to check the number associated with the lexeme. In all bible modules in this collections, except the phono version ETCBCp(k).SQLite, you can read article on ditionary lookup of each word, where you can find corresponding Hebrew lexemes and numbers assigned to them. For example, you will get קרא and H70027 with ETCBC dictionary on clicking יִּקְרָ֨א in Genesis 1:5. If you turn on display for Strong's number you can simply find 70027 right next to word.

Search for words based on particular lexeme(s):
To search all variants of a particular lexeme, say, קרא , simply use Strong's number search function available in MyBile, by clicking the number 70027 or typing manually.
You may also search multiple words appear in same verses, say, try to search for H70003 H70004. (please note that a space is requited between separate nubmers in the search field)

Search for lexeme together with morphological data:
To search for lexeme together with morphological data, say יִּקְרָ֨א in Genesis 1:5 is based on lexeme קרא and its full morphology is "verb, qal, wayyiqtol, third person, masculine, singular", simply click the 8-digit numbers next to the 5-digit numbers and click search. With this you will get all verses having variants of קרא tagged with morphological data "verb, qal, wayyiqtol, third person, masculine, singular".
Remarks: You can also do it in ETCBC ditionary, by clicking linked morphology in analytical lexicon and proceed the search.

Filtering on searching morphological data:
You can do partial moropholgy search with ETCBCq(k).SQLite3, few examples below:
- to search all words of קרא ONLY in verbal stem pual OR nif, use "word search" and type "L70027.verb.pual | L70027.verb.nif" and proceed the search.
- to search all words of קרא ONLY in all verbal stems EXCEPT qal, use "word search" and type "L70027.verb & ~ L70027.verb.qal" and proceed the search.
- to search all words of קרא ONLY in participle forms, use "word search" and type "L70027.verb.____.ptca" and proceed the search.
- to search all words of קרא ONLY in participle AND plural forms, use "word search" and type "L70027.verb.____.ptca.__._.pl" and proceed the search.
Remarks:
- Searching codes are available when users click "CODES" in beginning of each verse.
- To read more on searching possiblities in MyBible app, please go to http://mybible.zone/search-eng.php

ETCBC dictionary (installed separately)

File: ETCBC4c.dictionary.SQLite3

ETCBC data:
- idealized lexeme (linked)
- realized lexeme (linked)
- language, part-of-speech (word), lexical set & type of named entity
- English gloss
- analytical lexicon (all manuscript forms of the same lexical form; with full morphological information)
- ETCBC morphology (word & pronominal suffix)
- phrase-dependent part-of-speech in morphology
- data on morphemes (nominal ending, preformative, pronominal suffix, univalent final, verbal ending, root formation)

Extra materials:
- "strong's exhaustive concordance" added (formatted from this source: github.com/openscriptures/strongs)
- links to various dictionaries added by Eliran Wong
- two-way links between Strong's numbers and ETCBC data added by Eliran Wong
- work with Strong's no. replacement feature in MyBible (version 4.6+)

Notes on lexemes:
https://shebanq.ancient-data.org/shebanq/static/docs/featuredoc/features/comments/vocalized_lexeme.html
The values of vocalized lexeme look like the values of the g_lex_utf8 feature of word occurrences, but they are not the same. The vocalized lexeme is an idealized (aka paradigmatic), pointed representation of the lexeme, which may or may not occur in the text. The g_lex_utf8 is the realized lexeme in a concrete occurrence, of which it may be a part.

Notes on part-of-speech:
Users may find of part-of-speech information appeared in morphology different from part-of-speech information in lexical entry. Part-of-speech information in morphology are phrase dependent part-of-speech. You may read more below on "phrase dependent part-of-speech".

Notes on phrase dependent part-of-speech:
https://etcbc.github.io/text-fabric-data/features/hebrew/etcbc4c/pdp.html
The part of speech that is assigned to a word based on its phrase context rather than on its lexical identity.
